12.3. Interrogation

PTS cs 638 The four jhānas: Herein a monk aloof from sense pleasures, aloof from unskilful dhammas, attains and dwells in the first jhāna accompanied by initial application, accompanied by sustained application, with rapture and pleasure born of detachment. Inhibiting initial application and sustained application, he attains and dwells in the second jhāna with internal refinement, exalted development of mind, without initial application, without sustained application, with rapture and pleasure born of samādhi. Desireless of rapture he dwells equable, mindful, aware, and he experiences pleasure by way of mental dhammas; this the Noble Ones declare, “The equable mindful dweller in pleasure”; he attains and dwells in the third jhāna. By the abandoning of pleasure and by the abandoning of pain, then first terminating mental pleasure and mental pain he attains and dwells in the fourth jhāna (which is) neither-pain-nor-pleasure (but is) purity of mindfulness caused by equanimity.
